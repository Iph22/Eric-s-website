About Me

Eric Darko is an accomplished coach distinguished by both his educational background and his proven track record of success in the field. He commenced his education at New Hope School in Ashaley Botwe and later attended Adisadel College in Cape Coast, where he successfully completed the West African Senior School Certificate Examination (WASSCE). Mr. Darko furthered his academic pursuits at the University of Education, Winneba, earning a Bachelor of Science degree in Health, Physical Education, Sports, and Recreation. Additionally, he attended Missionsoft Computer School in Lapaz, where he became a Certified Software Developer. He also holds a Level 1 Diploma in Introduction to Basketball Coaching from the International Basketball University in Lithuania.

Demonstrating strong leadership abilities early in his career, Mr. Darko served as School Prefect from 2000 to 2001. His coaching career encompasses significant roles, including his tenure at Royalties Sports Academy from January 6, 2014, to January 7, 2017, where he functioned as the Director of Physical Education. He began his coaching journey as an Assistant Basketball Coach at Presbyterian Boys Secondary School in Legon. Before he engaged with Royalties Sports Academy, he was persuaded by Mike Davies to join the institution after achieving a second-place finish in the Sprite Ball Basketball Championship. Furthermore, Mr. Darko coached Galaxy International School to a third-place finish at the Independent Schools Sports Association Games (ISSAG) in January 2019, departing from Galaxy in 2021.

In 2020, he co-founded the Cedar Oak Basketball Academy (COBA) alongside Coach Albert Ato Sam and Coach Benjamin Nkansah Antwi on September 24. Most recently, Mr. Darko led the COBA Girls to victory in the COBA Fourth Anniversary Tournament. He has also organized various basketball camps for different age groups and has trained some of the most talented basketball players in the nation.

His coaching accomplishments include consecutive interschool basketball competition victories at Presbyterian Boys Secondary School in 2011 and 2012, a second-place finish at the Sprite Ball Basketball Championship in 2014, and another second-place finish at the Elite U16 Tournament in 2023 held in Jamestown. His commitment and expertise have established him as one of the most sought-after coaches in Ghana.
