About Me

Eric Darko is a dedicated basketball coach and sports professional committed to elevating basketball’s profile in Ghana, particularly among youth and young females. Eric combines his academic background with hands-on coaching experience to nurture talent and promote sportsmanship. He commenced his education at New Hope School in Ashaley Botwe and later attended Adisadel College in Cape Coast, where he successfully completed the West African Senior School Certificate Examination (WASSCE). Mr. Darko furthered his academic pursuits at the University of Education, Winneba, earning a Bachelor of Science degree in Health, Physical Education, Sports and Recreation. Additionally, he attended Missionsoft Computer School in Lapaz, where he became a Certified Software Developer. He also holds a Level 1 Diploma in Introduction to Basketball Coaching from the International Basketball University in Lithuania. Eric is about to pursue a Certificate in Sports Psychology from National Sports College in Winneba which starts in August.

Demonstrating strong leadership abilities early in his career, Mr. Darko served as School Prefect from 2000 to 2001. His coaching career encompasses significant roles, including his tenure at Royalties Sports Academy from January 6, 2014, to January 7, 2017, where he functioned as the Director of Physical Education. He began his coaching journey as an Assistant Basketball Coach at Presbyterian Boys Secondary School in Legon. Before he engaged with Royalties Sports Academy, he was persuaded by Mike Davies to join the institution after achieving a second-place finish in the Sprite Ball Basketball Championship in 2014 and also after winning consecutive interschool Basketball Championships in 2012 and 2013 with the same school.

Furthermore, Mr. Darko coached Galaxy International School to a third-place finish at the Independent Schools Sports Association Games (ISSAG) in January 2019, departing from Galaxy in 2021. In 2020, he co-founded the Cedar Oak Basketball Academy (COBA) alongside Coach Albert Ato Sam and Coach Benjamin Nkansah Antwi on September 24. Most recently, Mr. Darko led the COBA Girls to victory in the COBA Fourth Anniversary Tournament in 2024 and another second-place finish with the U16 team of the Academy at the Elite U16 Tournament held in Jamestown in January, 2023. He has also organized various basketball camps for different age groups and has trained some of the most talented basketball players in the nation. His commitment and expertise have established him as one of the most sought-after Coaches in Ghana having helped over 20 student athletes gain athletic scholarships to different high schools abroad through his Basketball Academy. Eric has successfully positioned the sport to attract increased attention from corporate institutions, thereby improving its visibility and appeal.

He collaborates closely with the Ghana Basketball Association, other Basketball Academies and, leveraging these partnerships to develop grassroots programs and support youth engagement in sports. Beyond his coaching achievements, Eric is known for his unwavering dedication to the well-being of those he mentors. He goes the extra mile to provide support, guidance, and encouragement, fostering both physical fitness and personal growth among his athletes and community.
